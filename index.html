Beta, CAPM & Cost of Capital
Regression Beta · Bottom-Up Beta · EM Risk Premium · WACC for Turkish Stocks
Wks 1–2 · ~12 hrs Priority Gap ▼
Why You Missed It & Why It Matters
Beta is defined as β = Cov(Rᵢ, Rₘ) / Var(Rₘ) — the OLS slope of a stock's excess return regressed on the market's excess return. You know this math. The miss was likely on the interpretation: what β = 1.5 means for expected return via the Security Market Line, or how to compute a bottom-up beta for a BIST company with thinly-traded shares where regression beta is noisy and unreliable. For Turkish investors, understanding the full chain — risk-free rate → equity risk premium → country risk premium → beta → cost of equity → WACC — is essential for any DCF valuation.
🔬 Your Existing Skill Maps Here: Beta estimation is OLS with one regressor. You already know how to run this, interpret residuals, and understand confidence intervals. The financial layer to add: (1) why monthly data over 5 years is convention, (2) what the intercept (Jensen's alpha) means, (3) why for BIST stocks you should prefer bottom-up beta constructed from industry peers rather than your stock's thin trading history.
🇹🇷 BIST-Specific Problem: Many BIST stocks trade infrequently. Regression beta calculated from daily/weekly BIST data suffers from staleness bias — the "Scholes-Williams" or "Dimson" corrections exist for this. Damodaran's bottom-up approach (sector beta → un-lever → re-lever for your company's capital structure) bypasses this and is more reliable. His emerging market beta datasets include Turkey sector betas updated annually.

Equity Valuation & DCF — Cross-Market Comparison
DCF · EV/EBITDA · P/E Adjusted for EM · BIST 8× vs S&P 22× — Are They Comparable?
Wks 3–4 · ~12 hrs Core Skill ▼
Why This Matters
The mechanics of DCF — discounting future cash flows — is just NPV from your engineering background. The financial complexity is in the inputs: what to use as FCF (FCFF vs FCFE), how to handle terminal value (Gordon Growth vs Exit Multiple), and critically how to set the discount rate for a Turkish company with TRY revenues and USD debt. The cross-market P/E comparison is a conceptual trap: a BIST stock at 8× P/E in a 60% inflation environment may be more expensive in real terms than an S&P 500 stock at 22× P/E.
🇹🇷 EM Adjustment Required: Damodaran's January 2026 data shows Turkey's equity risk premium at ~10–12%, significantly higher than the US (~5.5%). A DCF for a Turkish company must embed this via a higher discount rate — which mechanically lowers the fair value. Many retail BIST investors ignore this and wonder why their "cheap" stocks stay cheap. Your engineering intuition about discount rates should make this click immediately.

Portfolio Construction & Risk Management
Markowitz · Efficient Frontier · BIST/SPX Correlation · Currency Risk · Position Sizing
Wks 5–7 · ~15 hrs High Value ▼
Why This Matters
Mean-variance optimisation is a quadratic programme: minimise portfolio variance subject to a return target constraint and weights summing to 1. You can solve this right now using scipy.optimize or CVXPY. The financial content to add: covariance matrix estimation (sample vs shrinkage), the efficient frontier, why in-sample optimisation over-fits (directly connects to your statistics background), and how TRY/USD correlation affects your total portfolio return when you hold both BIST and S&P 500.
🇹🇷 Hidden Risk: Your banking salary correlates with Turkish macro — if Turkey enters a severe recession or lira crisis, your income and your BIST holdings fall simultaneously. True portfolio thinking means treating your future salary stream as a TRY-denominated bond in the optimisation. This typically implies holding less BIST and more USD-denominated assets than a naive allocation would suggest.

Factor Investing & Empirical Asset Pricing
Fama-French 5-Factor · AQR Datasets · Factor Zoo · BIST Factor Evidence
Wks 8–10 · ~15 hrs High Value ▼
Why This Matters
Factor investing bridges your statistics background to portfolio management. A factor is just a systematic variable that explains cross-sectional return differences across stocks — exactly the kind of regression/ML problem you already work with. The Fama-French 5-factor model (Market, Size, Value, Profitability, Investment) is a multiple regression of stock returns on portfolio sorts. Your ML background makes the critical extension — ML for alpha research and cross-validation to avoid overfitting — directly applicable.
🔬 Critical Connection — "Factor Zoo": Harvey & Liu (2019) documented 400+ published factors. Your statistics training immediately raises the right question: with this many variables tested against the same data, most discoveries are false positives. The Bonferroni correction and familywise error rates apply directly. This is the most important methodological insight in modern empirical finance — and you already have the tools to understand it rigorously.

Behavioural Finance & Decision Architecture
Protect your analytical edge from your own biases
Wks 13–14 · ~10 hrs High Leverage ▼
Why This Matters
Analytical ability creates a specific behavioural risk: rationalization. The ability to build convincing models makes it easier to justify staying in losing positions ("my DCF is right, the market is wrong"). For dual-market investors, additional biases appear: home bias toward BIST due to familiarity, recency bias from Turkey's recent inflation surge, and TRY-denominated anchoring that makes USD returns look smaller than they are.
